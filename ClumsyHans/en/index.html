H. C. Andersen
Hans Christian Andersen (born 2 April 1805 in Odense, died 4 August 1875 at Rolighed in Copenhagen) was a Danish poet and writer who is world famous for his adventures. He was known for his bouquets and paper clips, and is believed to have invented the braided Danish Christmas heart. He is one of the protagonists of the Danish Golden Age. He was also the inventor of Tingseventyr, which is a subgenre of art adventure.
