H. C. Andersen
Hans Christian Andersen (born 2nd of April 1805 in Odense, died 4th of August 1875 at Rolighed in Copenhagen) was a Danish poet and author who is world famous for his fairy tales. He was known for his fairy tales, but also origami, as he is believed to have invented the Danish braided Christmas heart. He is one of the most known people of the Danish Golden Age (term for the Danish period which extends from the 18 hundreds to the 1850s).
